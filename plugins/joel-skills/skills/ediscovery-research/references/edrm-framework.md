# EDRM framework reference

Current model: **EDRM 2.0 (2023)** — published by EDRM (edrm.net), licensed CC BY 4.0 (attribute as "EDRM (edrm.net)"). The model is iterative, not a rigid sequence; stages overlap and loop in real matters. Verified against edrm.net 2026-07-06.

## The nine stages

1. **Information Governance** — managing ESI from creation to disposition so the organization is discovery-ready; reduces volume and risk before any matter exists. Companion framework: IGRM (below).
2. **Identification** — locating potential sources of relevant ESI: custodians, systems, date ranges; scoping what exists and where.
3. **Preservation** — protecting ESI against alteration or destruction, chiefly via **legal hold**. Failure risk: spoliation sanctions under FRCP 37(e).
4. **Collection** — gathering ESI in a defensible, forensically sound manner that preserves metadata and chain of custody.
5. **Processing** — reducing volume and converting to reviewable form: expansion of containers, metadata/text extraction, deduplication, deNIST, filtering. (Joel's home turf.)
6. **Review** — evaluating documents for relevance and privilege; linear review, TAR/predictive coding, and now GenAI-assisted review (e.g., aiR for Review).
7. **Analysis** — evaluating ESI for content and context: key patterns, topics, people, timelines. Runs throughout, not just after review.
8. **Production** — delivering ESI to other parties in agreed formats (native, image + load file), with Bates numbering, confidentiality endorsements, redactions.
9. **Presentation** — displaying ESI at deposition, hearing, trial.

## Related EDRM frameworks and tools

- **IGRM** (Information Governance Reference Model) — links legal, IT, records, privacy, and business stakeholders around information lifecycle responsibility.
- **Metrics Model** — standard vocabulary for measuring eDiscovery volume, time, and cost; useful for project-management questions.
- **Privacy & Security Risk Reduction Model** — reducing data risk across the lifecycle.
- **EDRM datasets** (e.g., Enron set) — public test data; safe to reference in samples and demos.
- Budget calculators, Security Audit Questionnaire, Maturity Self-Assessment (eMSAT) — all at edrm.net.

## Cross-cutting concepts worth knowing cold

- **Proportionality** — FRCP 26(b)(1): discovery scope balanced against burden/benefit. Underlies most scoping disputes.
- **Defensibility** — documented, repeatable, explainable process at every stage; the through-line of the whole model.
- **Meet and confer / 26(f) conference** — where ESI protocol, formats, and TAR use get negotiated.
- **TAR 1.0 vs 2.0 (CAL)** — one-time trained classifier vs. continuous active learning; Grossman–Cormack glossary is the standard vocabulary source.
- **Cross-border / international discovery** — GDPR and blocking statutes vs. US discovery obligations; a CEDS exam favorite.
