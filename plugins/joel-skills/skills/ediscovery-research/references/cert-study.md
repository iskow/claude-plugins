# Cert study reference — PM Specialist + CEDS

Exam facts below were verified 2026-07-06. Exam structures change — re-fetch the official source before a study sprint or registration.

## Relativity Project Management Specialist (target: 2026)

Source of truth: [official Study Resources PDF](https://help.relativity.com/Relativity/Certification/ProjectManagement/Relativity%20-%20Project%20Management%20Specialist%20-%20Study%20Resources.pdf) (last modified 2026-03-09) and the [cert page](https://www.relativity.com/resources/certification/specialist/relativity-project-management-specialist/).

**Logistics:** RelativityOne-based. Up to 75 items (some unscored), 75 minutes, $250, online or testing center. Feedback: pass/fail. Widely cited passing threshold is 80% [ASSUMPTION: from Relativity FAQ summaries, not restated in the 2026 PDF — confirm at registration]. Maintenance: 6 RCE credits/year. Suggested prep: 30+ hours; 3-month weekly study plan on learning.relativity.com.

**Structure:**
- **Part 1 — Scenarios (29%):** four scenarios × five questions; answers lock on submit. Workflows: (1) import and manage data, (2) manage review workflows, (3) perform linear review, (4) prepare and execute a production.
- **Part 2 — up to 55 MCQs**, revisitable. Weightings:

| Objective | Weight | High-importance items |
|---|---|---|
| Prepare Workspace Data | 23% | Use Processing |
| Facilitate & Monitor Review | 35% | Search operations; design review workflows |
| Production administration | 13% | Run Productions |

Medium: data management options, Review Center queue, structured analytics. Low: Import data, Collect use cases, aiR for Review, conceptual analytics, Export Data.

**Study priorities for Joel:** his Processing/Production depth is already strong (day job + RCA specialties). The 35% review block — search operations, review workflow design, Review Center — is the highest-weight area and the least like his daily staging/production work. Drill there first.

## ACEDS CEDS (target: after PM Specialist)

Source of truth: [aceds.org/ceds-certifications](https://aceds.org/ceds-certifications/) and the CEDS Candidate Handbook.

**Logistics:** 145 four-option MCQs (125 scored); passing ≈69.6% per third-party accounts [ASSUMPTION: confirm in current Candidate Handbook]. Kryterion testing centers worldwide, year-round. Packages: Standard $1,495 (1 attempt) / Complete $1,995 (2 attempts) — both include prep course, exam manual, practice questions, 1-year ACEDS membership, 2-year certification. Scenario-based questions, vendor-neutral.

**Domains** span the full EDRM lifecycle plus: project planning, litigation hold implementation, TAR, international/cross-border discovery, and ethics. CEDS is law-heavier than Relativity exams — FRCP, Sedona Principles, and case-law concepts matter as much as technology.

**Study priorities for Joel:** technology domains are largely covered by his experience. The gaps to drill: US legal framework (FRCP 26/34/37(e), proportionality, spoliation), ethics rules, international discovery (GDPR vs. US discovery), and left-side EDRM (information governance, identification, preservation) that data-ops roles rarely touch.

## Quiz-mode rules

1. **Match the real format.** PM Specialist: scenario sets of 5 questions plus standalone 4-option MCQs, weighted per the table above. CEDS: scenario-based 4-option MCQs across domains.
2. **Ground in docs.** Build questions from current official documentation; cite the source article in every answer explanation. Never use third-party dumps.
3. **Explain the why.** Every answer explanation covers why the right answer is right *and* why the distractors are wrong — Joel retains concepts, not answer keys.
4. **Track weak areas.** Note topics missed across sessions; weight the next quiz toward them.
5. **Flashcards:** Relativity's own guidance says create your own set, don't reuse others'. Generate candidate term/concept lists from study-plan topics, but have Joel phrase the cards — the phrasing is the learning.
6. **Exam-depth summaries:** for a "summarize X at exam depth" request, match topic importance — High = functionality + when/why to use it in a workflow; Medium = general understanding; Low = basic recognition.
