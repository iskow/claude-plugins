# Joel's Personal Brand

This skill keeps everything Joel makes looking and sounding consistent — one brand across all his projects. Apply it to any visual or written deliverable he creates.

## How to use this skill

1. **Pick a theme.** Joel has three brand directions (below). If he hasn't named one for this piece, ask: "Which theme — Cairn, Tessera, or Operant?" Once he locks a default, use that unless he says otherwise.
2. **Apply the palette by role**, not at random — each color has a job (anchor, secondary, soft neutral, accent, background). See the rules section.
3. **Use the type system** (Space Grotesk / Inter / JetBrains Mono) and the **voice** (concise, direct, warm).
4. **Stay minimalist** — generous whitespace, few sizes, one accent per view. Restraint is the brand.

## Typography (all free / Google Fonts)
- **Headings:** Space Grotesk — geometric, modern.
- **Body:** Inter — clean, highly readable.
- **Code / data / labels:** JetBrains Mono — for hex codes, metrics, file names, technical labels.

In web/HTML outputs, load these from Google Fonts. In Office files (docx/pptx) where the fonts may not be installed, fall back to a clean sans (e.g., a system sans) and keep the hierarchy — the goal is the look, not a hard dependency. Use few sizes, lots of whitespace, sentence case (never Title Case or ALL CAPS).

## Voice & tone
Carry Joel's writing style into any copy:
- Lead with the point or the ask in the first line.
- Concise and direct — if a word can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
- Warm but not salesy; plain words ("use" not "utilize").
- Short paragraphs. Match the channel and the client's English variant (US by default; UK/Commonwealth for UK/AU/NZ clients).
- Avoid the words "genuinely," "honestly," "straightforward."

## Application rules (why these matter)

- **Text in the anchor color on the light background.** This is the highest-contrast pairing and keeps body copy readable (aim for WCAG AA). Don't put small body text on the soft-neutral or accent colors.
- **One accent per view.** The accent (Terracotta / Amber / Orchid) is for the single thing you want the eye to land on — a primary button, an active link, a key stat. Overusing it kills the minimalist feel.
- **Soft neutral = surfaces, not text.** Use Sand / Sage / Lilac Mist for cards, section backgrounds, and dividers.
- **Charts:** order series with the anchor first, then secondary, then accent for the series that matters most; keep the rest in muted neutrals so the important line stands out.
- **Whitespace is part of the brand.** When in doubt, add space and remove elements.

## Examples

**Example 1 — styling a report/doc header (Cairn):**
Title in Space Grotesk, Slate Pine, on a Parchment page; a thin Walnut rule under it; one Terracotta accent on the section label. Body in Inter, Slate Pine.

**Example 2 — a primary button (Operant):**
Royal Violet fill, Veil (near-white) text, rounded corners; the only violet-saturated element in that view so it reads as the action.

**Example 3 — a metric callout (Tessera):**
Big number in JetBrains Mono, Deep Teal, on a Mist card; label in Steel Teal; if it's the hero stat, color just that number in Warm Amber.

## Notes
- These three themes are intentional placeholders while Joel decides on a final brand name. Treat them as interchangeable "skins" — same type and voice, different color world.
- When Joel locks a single direction, this skill can be trimmed to that one theme.
